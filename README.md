
# Circular Battery Systems for Mongolia

## Executive Overview

Mongolia is entering an early but accelerating phase of electric vehicle (EV) adoption.  
This project assesses the long-term implications of EV growth on battery waste volumes and industrial recycling capacity planning.

---

## Project Objective

To determine:

- When EV battery waste will emerge at scale
- What recycling capacity is economically justified
- Whether immediate large-scale investment is viable
- What phased infrastructure pathway minimizes financial risk

---

## Analytical Structure

1. EV Adoption Forecast (Low / Base / High)
2. Historical EV Stock Baseline (2015–2025)
3. Battery End-of-Life Waste Projection (2030–2040)
4. Plant Capacity Scenario Modeling
5. Financial Feasibility Assessment
6. Regulatory & EPR Mapping

---

## Key Findings

- Battery waste remains moderate until ~2033
- Immediate 5,000 t industrial plant is not justified
- 1,500 t preprocessing hub is economically viable
- Phased scaling reduces capital exposure and market risk

---

## Strategic Recommendation

Phase 1 (2030–2033):
Deploy modular preprocessing hub (1,000–1,500 t/year)

Phase 2 (Post-2033):
Scale only if sustained waste exceeds 3,000 t/year
